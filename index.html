Who

West Side National Park is managed by the Bahamas National Trust. According to their website, the BNT is a "non-profit, non-government membership organization that relies on donatins to continue conserving and protecting the Bahamian environment." The BNT is managed by a group of 29 members, 14 of whom are elected, and 15 of whom are appointed. The council is home to 6 PhD holders. They are funded by a wide range of both individual and corperate donors such as the Bahamas Telecommunications Company and The Owl Foundation.

What

West Side National Park is a mixed protected area, including both marine and terrestrial habitats. Encompassing 1.5 million acres, this national park is home to an exceptionally wide range of wildlife.

West Side National Park is home to a wide range of birds, reptiles, bugs, and sea creatures. Of those species, several are listed as endangered, including the Bahama Oriole (pictured). The Bahama Oriole is considered "critically endangered." Other species include the Piping Plover, the Bahaman Rock Iguana, the Queen Conch, the West Indian Flamingo, Smalltooth Sawfish, and Sea Turtules.
